How it works

Four layers, one device

Everything runs locally. No message ever leaves your child's phone.

1

Custom Keyboard

A native iOS keyboard extension monitors typed and received messages across all apps. Runs as a secure sandboxed process.

2

On-Device AI

Multi-language NLP and Apple NaturalLanguage framework analyse text in real time. Regex patterns + ML classifiers score risk across 7 threat categories.

3

Smart Alerts

Parents receive context-aware alerts with risk levels — not raw messages. The child's privacy stays intact. Scores range from low to critical.

4

Child Intervention

Age-appropriate pop-ups give the child guidance in the moment. Crisis situations surface local hotlines immediately. Education, not punishment.

Research Programme

Zero data by default. Opt-in research by consent.

The app collects nothing. The research study — run by university partners — collects anonymised outcomes from consenting participants only.

How participants join the study

1. Recruited by university partners

Participants are recruited through consortium universities across Denmark, Norway, Germany, Austria, and the Netherlands. Not through the app. Not through ads.

Recruitment via institutions

2. Informed consent + ethics approval

Each participant (and parent, for minors) signs IRB/ethics-approved informed consent. They know exactly what is collected, how it's stored, and how to withdraw at any time.

IRB-approved per country

3. Research code entered in app

Participants receive a unique study code from their research coordinator. Entering the code in the app unlocks the research module — a separate data pipeline that runs alongside the normal zero-data app.

Opt-in only

4. App sends anonymised metadata

The research module sends alert metadata (category + score + frequency) and in-app survey responses. Never message content. Never photos. Never names. Every data point is pseudonymised at the device level before transmission.

No message content ever leaves the device

5. Wellbeing surveys delivered in-app

Standardised clinical instruments (GAD-7, PHQ-A, SHQ, PSQI, RSES) are delivered as in-app questionnaires at scheduled intervals — weekly, monthly, and quarterly over 24 months.

Standard clinical instruments

6. Participant withdraws at any time

One tap deletes the research code. Data pipeline stops immediately. Previously submitted data can be requested for deletion under GDPR Article 17.

Full withdrawal rights

What the study collects (and what it doesn't)

✓

Alert metadata

Category (grooming, bullying, etc.), risk score, frequency, time of day. No message text.

✓

Wellbeing survey responses

GAD-7 (anxiety), PHQ-A (depression), SHQ (self-harm), PSQI (sleep), RSES (self-esteem).

✓

Aggregate screen time

Total daily hours, app category breakdown, late-night usage flags. Not specific app names.

✓

Demographics (anonymised)

Age bracket, country, gender. No names, addresses, school names, or device identifiers.

✗

Never collected

Message content, photos, contacts, location, browsing history, app-specific data, device IDs, IP addresses.

Where the data lives

University data controllers Each country's lead university is the GDPR data controller
EU-hosted research infrastructure EBRAINS / EUDAT — sovereign European data platforms
End-to-end encrypted transit TLS 1.3 from device → research endpoint. No intermediaries.
Pseudonymised at source Device generates random participant ID. No link to identity.
Key distinction: Guardeon (the non-profit) never touches research data. University partners are the data controllers. Guardeon builds the instrument; universities run the study. This separation is by design — it means even if Guardeon were compromised, no research data would be at risk.
Partnerships & Research

We don't work alone

Guardeon is built on partnerships with the organisations leading the fight against child exploitation, online violence, and digital harm.

Technology

Thorn (Safer API)

Integrating Thorn's Safer hash-matching technology to detect known child sexual abuse material (CSAM) on-device — the gold standard in proactive image detection, trusted by platforms worldwide.

Technology

Microsoft PhotoDNA

Perceptual hashing that identifies known harmful images even when cropped, resized, or altered. A critical second layer alongside Thorn for image-based threats.

Reporting

NCMEC CyberTipline

Direct reporting pipeline to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children. When Guardeon detects confirmed CSAM, parents can escalate to law enforcement in one tap.

Research

Horizon Europe Consortium

Leading an EUR 8M research consortium across 5 EU countries — clinical psychology, computational linguistics, child rights, and on-device AI. 48-month programme with a 3,000-participant longitudinal study.

Research

University Partners

Collaborating with research institutions across Denmark, Norway, Germany, Austria, and the Netherlands on neuroimaging studies, multilingual NLP, and child digital wellbeing.

Standards & Policy

IEEE & EU Regulatory

Contributing to IEEE standards for youth digital safety. Aligned with the EU AI Act, Digital Services Act (Article 28), and Denmark's under-15 social media legislation.

Child Safety

IWF & INHOPE

Working with the Internet Watch Foundation and INHOPE network for access to verified hash databases and international CSAM reporting infrastructure across 50+ countries.

Government

Municipalities & Schools

Pilot programmes with Danish municipalities and school districts. Anonymised aggregate dashboards give institutions early-warning capability without exposing individual student data.

Crisis Services

National Hotlines

Integrated crisis pathways to Børnetelefonen (DK 116 111), Red Barnet, ForældreTelefonen, and international equivalents. When a child is at risk, the right help is one tap away.
